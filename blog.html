Writing Tips & Best Practices

Quick Readability Checklist

  • Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
  • Use active voice instead of passive voice
  • Choose common words over complex alternatives
  • Break up long paragraphs into shorter ones
  • Use headings and subheadings to organize content
  • Include bullet points and numbered lists
  • Add examples to illustrate complex concepts
  • Test your content with real users

Tools for Better Writing

Beyond readability analysis, consider these complementary tools:

  • Grammar checkers: Ensure technical correctness
  • Style guides: Maintain consistency across content
  • User testing: Get real feedback on comprehension
  • A/B testing: Compare different versions of content

Industry-Specific Guidelines

Different industries have different readability requirements:

  • Healthcare: 6th-8th grade level for patient materials
  • Legal: Plain language initiatives require 8th-10th grade level
  • Education: Match content to student grade levels
  • Technology: Balance technical accuracy with accessibility
  • Finance: Regulatory requirements for clear communication
